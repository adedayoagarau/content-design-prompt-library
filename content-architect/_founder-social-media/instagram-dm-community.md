### DM Strategy Architecture

**Three DM Types to Manage:**

**1. Inbound DMs (People messaging you)**
- Engagement replies (reactions to Stories/posts)
- Questions about product/service
- Collaboration requests
- Fan messages/appreciation
- Spam/sales pitches

**2. Proactive Outreach (You initiating)**
- Strategic relationship building
- Partnership opportunities
- Customer success check-ins
- Re-engagement of dormant followers

**3. Automated Sequences (Tool-triggered)**
- Welcome message for new followers
- Resource delivery ("DM 'TEMPLATE' for...")
- FAQ auto-responses
- Qualification funnels

### Response Tiering System

**Tier 1 - VIP/High-Priority (<2 hour response)**:
- Active investors in your pipeline
- Paying customers or hot prospects
- Strategic partners
- Press/media
- Key hires in recruitment

**Tier 2 - Warm Leads (<24 hour response)**:
- Qualified prospects asking about product
- Collaboration inquiries (vetted)
- Community champions
- Talent candidates

**Tier 3 - General Engagement (<48 hour response)**:
- Genuine questions
- Positive feedback
- Content engagement
- General networking

**Tier 4 - Low Priority (Batch response or ignore)**:
- Generic "great post!" messages
- Spam/sales pitches
- Off-topic requests
- Inappropriate messages

### Inbound DM Response Framework

**The "RAPID" Response Model:**

**R - Read and Categorize**
- Quickly assess tier and intent
- Tag in CRM if business-critical

**A - Acknowledge Promptly**
- Even if can't fully respond yet
- "Thanks for reaching out! Will reply fully by [time]"

**P - Personalize**
- Use their name
- Reference their specific question/comment
- Avoid obvious copy-paste (even with templates)

**I - Inform or Invite**
- Answer their question thoroughly
- OR invite to next step (call, email, product demo)

**D - Direct Next Action**
- Clear CTA ("Book a time here:" / "Check out [link]")
- Don't leave conversation hanging

### Proactive Outreach Strategy

**When to Cold DM (Strategically):**

**DO Reach Out When:**
- Genuine reason to connect (shared interest, mutual connection, relevant content)
- Can offer specific value (not just "let's connect")
- They've engaged with your content (warm-ish)
- Clear alignment with your goals (partnership fit, customer profile, etc.)

**DON'T Reach Out When:**
- Generic mass DM campaign
- Pure self-promotion
- No personalization
- They've ignored previous attempts

**Cold DM Framework (The "VALUE-FIRST" Approach):**

**Daily DM Workflow (Time-Boxed):**

**Morning (15-20 min):**
- Check Tier 1 VIP DMs (respond immediately)
- Flag Tier 2 for later response
- Archive/ignore Tier 4

**Midday (10-15 min):**
- Respond to Tier 2
- Engage with Story replies (this generates more engagement)

**End of Day (10-15 min):**
- Final Tier 1 check
- Batch respond to Tier 3
- Review automated sequences performance

**Weekly (30 min):**
- Review DM analytics (conversion rate, common questions)
- Update templates based on patterns
- Plan proactive outreach (5-10 strategic DMs/week)

## Best Practices

- **Personalize Everything**: Even templates should feel personal
- **Respond to Story Replies**: This generates more future DMs
- **Use Voice Notes**: For complex answers, more personal than long text
- **Move High-Value Off Platform**: DMs for connection, email/calls for deals
- **Track Everything**: You can't improve what you don't measure
- **Set Boundaries**: Politely redirect or decline low-fit requests
- **Automate Smartly**: Handle FAQs automatically, humans for nuance
- **Proactive > Reactive**: Don't just respond; strategically initiate
