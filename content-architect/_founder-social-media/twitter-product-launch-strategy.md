## Twitter Product Launch Strategy v1.0

**Purpose**: Orchestrate comprehensive Twitter/X product launch campaigns that maximize visibility, generate buzz, drive conversions, and create sustained momentum through real-time engagement, strategic content sequencing, and community mobilization.

**Context**: Use this framework when launching products, major features, rebrands, or significant company announcements where Twitter is a primary or supporting launch channel. Twitter's real-time, conversation-driven nature makes it ideal for building hype, managing launch day chaos, responding to feedback, and creating viral moments. This framework applies to B2B SaaS, developer tools, consumer apps, or any product where early adopters, tech community, or media are active on Twitter. Best for launches ranging from quiet beta releases to major public announcements requiring 4-8 weeks of campaign orchestration.

**Strategic Frameworks Applied**:
- **Launch Campaign Phasing** - Pre-launch hype, launch day execution, post-launch momentum
- **Real-Time Engagement Strategy** - Managing live conversation, feedback loops, and viral moments
- **FOMO Engineering** - Scarcity, exclusivity, and urgency tactics native to Twitter culture
- **Community Mobilization** - Orchestrating advocates, early adopters, and amplifiers
- **Controversy Navigation** - Handling criticism, competitors, and unexpected responses
- **Algorithmic Momentum** - Leveraging Twitter's recommendation and trending systems

## Variables

Define strategic variables for comprehensive Twitter launch campaign:

### Product & Launch Context Variables
- `{{product_name}}`: Name of product/feature being launched
- `{{launch_type}}`: New product | Major feature | Public beta | Rebrand | Pricing change | Acquisition
- `{{product_category}}`: Industry vertical and product type
- `{{unique_value_proposition}}`: Core problem solved and key differentiation
- `{{target_customer_profile}}`: Primary ICP (job titles, communities, interests)
- `{{launch_date}}`: Official public launch date and time
- `{{availability_model}}`: Public launch | Waitlist | Invite-only | Beta access | Freemium | Paid

### Campaign Objective Variables
- `{{primary_goal}}`: Signups | Downloads | Sales | Awareness | Media coverage | Community building
- `{{success_metrics}}`: Quantified targets (e.g., "10K signups in 48 hours", "Trending in tech category", "500 tweets using #ProductName")
- `{{secondary_goals}}`: Supporting objectives (investor visibility, talent attraction, partnership opportunities)
- `{{conversion_path}}`: How Twitter drives action (Direct link | Waitlist | Trial | Purchase | App store)

### Positioning & Messaging Variables
- `{{founder_narrative}}`: Personal story connecting founder to product
- `{{category_position}}`: Category creator | Disruptor | Better alternative | Evolution
- `{{competitive_angle}}`: How you position against existing solutions
- `{{messaging_pillars}}`: 3-5 core messages (e.g., speed, simplicity, affordability, innovation)
- `{{controversy_strategy}}`: Polarizing stance or safe positioning

### Timeline & Pacing Variables
- `{{pre_launch_duration}}`: Weeks before launch to start building awareness (2-6 weeks typical)
- `{{launch_day_intensity}}`: Tweet frequency on launch day (4-8 tweets + engagement)
- `{{post_launch_duration}}`: Weeks of sustained campaign (2-4 weeks)
- `{{milestone_dates}}`: Key moments (beta open, press embargo, pricing reveal, feature drops)

### Content & Creative Variables
- `{{content_mix}}`: Tweet types (Threads | Single tweets | Polls | Spaces | Videos | Memes)
- `{{visual_assets}}`: Product demos, screenshots, graphics, founder videos, testimonials
- `{{social_proof}}`: Beta users, testimonials, metrics, press quotes, investor/advisor endorsements
- `{{building_in_public}}`: Behind-scenes content, team stories, development journey

### Amplification & Distribution Variables
- `{{organic_amplification}}`: Community, employees, partners, beta users, influencers
- `{{paid_strategy}}`: Twitter Ads budget and targeting (if applicable)
- `{{media_strategy}}`: Journalist outreach, embargo coordination, press kit
- `{{influencer_engagement}}`: Tech voices, industry experts, micro-influencers
- `{{community_activation}}`: Twitter Spaces, AMAs, Discord/Slack cross-promotion

### Controversy & Crisis Management

**Handling Negative Feedback:**

**If constructive criticism:**
```
"This is fair feedback, [Name]. We made [decision] because [reasoning], but I hear you on [their concern]. We're exploring [potential solution]. Thanks for pushing us."
```

**If bug/issue:**
```
"You're right, this is broken. We're on it. Fix shipping in [timeframe]. DM me your email and I'll personally update you when it's resolved."
```

**If competitive attack:**
```
"Healthy competition makes everyone better. We're focused on serving [our customers] with [unique approach]. Plenty of room for different solutions to coexist."
```
(Don't take bait, stay above the fray)

**If going viral for wrong reasons:**
- Acknowledge issue quickly and transparently
- Take responsibility (don't blame, deflect, or delete)
- Share specific action plan
- Follow up when resolved

## Common Pitfalls

- **Announcing Too Early**: Hype dies before launch if timeline too long
- **Over-Promising**: Hype that product can't deliver on damages trust
- **Posting and Ghosting**: Not engaging with replies kills algorithmic boost
- **No Clear CTA**: People excited but don't know how to access product
- **Ignoring Negative Feedback**: Defensive responses backfire publicly
- **Team Radio Silence**: Founder tweets but team says nothing (looks bad)
- **No Post-Launch Plan**: Big launch then silence; momentum evaporates
- **Copying Others' Launch Playbook**: What worked for them may not work for you
- **Launch Day Only on Twitter**: Should be omnichannel (email, PH, blog, etc.)
- **No Metrics Transparency**: Building in public means sharing real numbers
