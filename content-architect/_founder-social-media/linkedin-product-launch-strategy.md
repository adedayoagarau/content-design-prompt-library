## LinkedIn Product Launch Strategy v1.0

**Purpose**: Design comprehensive, multi-phase LinkedIn launch campaigns that build anticipation, drive awareness, and create sustained momentum for product releases through strategic founder-led content, community engagement, and conversion optimization.

**Context**: Use this framework when planning a product launch (new product, major feature, rebrand, market expansion) where LinkedIn is a primary or supporting channel. Ideal for B2B SaaS, developer tools, enterprise software, or professional services where decision-makers are active on LinkedIn. This goes beyond a single announcement post to create an integrated 6-8 week campaign spanning pre-launch hype, launch day execution, and post-launch sustainability. Best for Series A+ companies with established audiences or pre-launch startups building early community.

**Strategic Frameworks Applied**:
- **Launch Campaign Architecture** - Pre-launch, launch, post-launch phases with distinct objectives
- **Narrative Momentum Building** - Creating anticipation through progressive revelation
- **Stakeholder Segmentation** - Tailored messaging for customers, prospects, investors, talent, press
- **Social Proof Cascades** - Orchestrating testimonials, case studies, and influencer validation
- **Conversion Funnel Optimization** - Moving from awareness to waitlist to active users
- **Platform Algorithm Leverage** - Maximizing LinkedIn's distribution during launch window

## Variables

Define strategic variables for comprehensive launch campaign:

### Product & Launch Context Variables
- `{{product_name}}`: Name of product/feature being launched
- `{{launch_type}}`: New product | Major feature | Rebrand | Market expansion | Pricing change | Partnership
- `{{product_category}}`: Industry vertical and product type
- `{{unique_value_proposition}}`: Core problem solved and differentiation (1-2 sentences)
- `{{target_customer_profile}}`: Primary ICP (job titles, company size, industry, pain points)
- `{{launch_date}}`: Official public launch date
- `{{availability}}`: General availability | Waitlist | Beta | Invite-only | Freemium | Paid-only

### Campaign Objective Variables
- `{{primary_goal}}`: Waitlist signups | Trial starts | Revenue | Brand awareness | Media coverage | Talent attraction
- `{{success_metrics}}`: Quantified targets (e.g., "1000 waitlist signups", "50 enterprise demos booked", "5 media mentions")
- `{{secondary_goals}}`: Supporting objectives (community building, investor visibility, partnership opportunities)
- `{{conversion_mechanism}}`: How LinkedIn drives action (Landing page | Product-led signup | Sales conversation | Waitlist)

### Audience & Positioning Variables
- `{{founder_positioning}}`: How founder's personal brand ties to product (technical credibility, market expertise, personal story)
- `{{customer_segments}}`: Primary, secondary, tertiary audience segments
- `{{competitor_landscape}}`: Existing solutions audience may be using or considering
- `{{category_positioning}}`: Best in category | Category creator | Disruptor | Evolution of existing
- `{{messaging_pillars}}`: 3-5 core messages to communicate (speed, simplicity, power, cost, unique approach)

### Campaign Timeline Variables
- `{{pre_launch_duration}}`: Weeks before launch to start campaign (typically 3-4 weeks)
- `{{launch_week_intensity}}`: Content frequency during launch week
- `{{post_launch_duration}}`: Weeks of sustained campaign post-launch (typically 2-3 weeks)
- `{{milestone_dates}}`: Key dates (beta open, press embargo lift, pricing announcement, etc.)

### Content & Creative Variables
- `{{content_formats}}`: Mix of text posts, carousels, videos, LinkedIn Live, articles, polls
- `{{visual_assets}}`: Product screenshots, demo videos, explainer graphics, customer testimonials
- `{{social_proof_elements}}`: Beta tester quotes, case study metrics, analyst validation, press logos
- `{{behind_scenes_content}}`: Building in public moments, team stories, design decisions

### Amplification Variables
- `{{organic_tactics}}`: Community engagement, employee advocacy, partner sharing, influencer tags
- `{{paid_budget}}`: Allocated spend for LinkedIn ads (if any)
- `{{partner_collaboration}}`: Launch partners, integration partners, co-marketing opportunities
- `{{pr_strategy}}`: Media outreach, embargo dates, press kit availability
- `{{influencer_engagement}}`: Industry voices to involve (early access, testimonials, co-creation)

## Prompt

You are a product launch strategist designing a comprehensive LinkedIn campaign for **{{product_name}}**, a **{{product_category}}** launching on **{{launch_date}}**. The campaign aims to achieve **{{primary_goal}}** with a success target of **{{success_metrics}}** by leveraging **{{founder_positioning}}** and reaching **{{target_customer_profile}}**.

### Campaign Architecture (3-Phase System)

**Phase 1: Pre-Launch Hype (Weeks -4 to -1)**

Objectives:
- Build anticipation and curiosity
- Educate market on problem space
- Establish founder as authority on the problem
- Collect early interest (waitlist, beta signups)
- Create FOMO through progressive revelation

**Phase 2: Launch Week (Week 0)**

Objectives:
- Maximize visibility and reach
- Drive conversion to {{conversion_mechanism}}
- Generate social proof and testimonials
- Secure media coverage and influencer mentions
- Create viral moments

**Phase 3: Post-Launch Momentum (Weeks +1 to +3)**

Objectives:
- Sustain conversation and engagement
- Share customer success stories
- Address objections and questions
- Iterate messaging based on market response
- Plan next milestone campaign

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch Content Calendar

**Week -4: Problem Agitation**

Focus: Establish the pain point {{unique_value_proposition}} solves, without mentioning product yet.

*Content Ideas:*

**Post 1: Personal Story**
- Hook: "I spent 6 months doing [painful task] manually. Then I realized [insight]."
- Content: Founder's personal experience with the problem
- Why it matters: Establishes credibility and relatability
- CTA: "Anyone else struggling with this?"
- Format: Text post with authentic photo
- Engagement Goal: High comment engagement, story-sharing

**Post 2: Market Research / Data**
- Hook: "We surveyed 500 [target customers]. 87% are wasting 10+ hours/week on [problem]."
- Content: Original research or compelling data on problem prevalence
- Why it matters: Quantifies pain, creates authority
- CTA: "Is this your experience? What's it costing you?"
- Format: Text + simple data visualization
- Engagement Goal: Validation comments, saves for reference

**Post 3: Existing Solutions Fall Short**
- Hook: "Current solutions for [problem]: ‚õî Too complex ‚õî Too expensive ‚õî Don't integrate with [tool]"
- Content: Analyze {{competitor_landscape}} gaps (not bashing, objective analysis)
- Why it matters: Primes market for better solution
- CTA: "What's missing from your current solution?"
- Format: Text post with bullet points
- Engagement Goal: Frustration sharing, pain point validation

**Week -3: Solution Teasing**

Focus: Hint at solution approach without full reveal.

**Post 4: First Tease**
- Hook: "What if [problem] could be solved in 60 seconds instead of 60 minutes?"
- Content: Introduce solution philosophy/approach at high level
- Include: Subtle product hint ("I've been working on something...")
- CTA: "Would this change how you work?"
- Format: Text post
- Engagement Goal: Curiosity, speculation in comments

**Post 5: Behind-the-Scenes**
- Hook: "Here's why we're building [product] differently:"
- Content: Design philosophy, technical approach, or unique methodology
- Why it matters: Builds anticipation, shows thoughtfulness
- CTA: "What matters most to you in a [product category] tool?"
- Format: Carousel showing design principles or tech architecture
- Engagement Goal: Preferences sharing, excitement building

**Post 6: Early Beta Results**
- Hook: "Our beta testers are seeing [surprising result]. Here's what they're saying:"
- Content: Anonymous beta tester quotes, early metrics, testimonials
- Social Proof: First introduction of validation
- CTA: "Want beta access? Drop a üëã in the comments."
- Format: Text with pulled quote graphic
- Engagement Goal: Beta signups, social proof, FOMO

**Week -2: Product Reveal**

Focus: Officially announce product, open waitlist/beta, build urgency.

**Post 7: Official Announcement**
- Hook: "Introducing [Product Name]: [One-line value proposition]"
- Content: What it is, who it's for, core differentiation
- Visuals: Product screenshot, logo, demo GIF
- CTA: "Join the waitlist: [link in comments]"
- Format: Text + visual + video teaser (30 sec)
- Engagement Goal: Shares, waitlist signups, press pickup
- Amplification: Consider $100-300 boost for reach

**Post 8: How It Works**
- Hook: "Here's how [Product Name] works (in 90 seconds):"
- Content: Quick walkthrough of core workflow
- Format: Video demo or carousel walkthrough
- CTA: "Questions? Ask me anything in comments."
- Engagement Goal: Saves, shares, clarification questions

**Post 9: Who It's For**
- Hook: "Built specifically for [ICP]. Here's why:"
- Content: Persona-specific benefits and use cases
- Testimonials: Early customer quote
- CTA: "Does this sound like you? Join waitlist: [link]"
- Format: Text post
- Engagement Goal: Self-identification, segment-specific sharing

**Week -1: Urgency & Scarcity**

Focus: Drive pre-launch signups, create urgency for launch day.

**Post 10: Countdown**
- Hook: "One week until [Product Name] launches. Here's what you need to know:"
- Content: Launch date, early bird benefits, what to expect
- Scarcity: "First 100 signups get [bonus]"
- CTA: "Set a reminder, don't miss this."
- Format: Text + countdown graphic
- Engagement Goal: Reminders set, final waitlist push

**Post 11: Customer Sneak Peek**
- Hook: "We gave [Industry Leader/Company] early access. Here's what happened:"
- Content: Detailed case study or testimonial from beta customer
- Results: Specific metrics (saved X hours, increased Y by Z%)
- CTA: "See it in action on launch day."
- Format: Carousel case study
- Engagement Goal: Credibility, aspiration, shares

**Post 12: Founder Note**
- Hook: "In 3 days, we're launching [Product]. Here's why I built this:"
- Content: Personal, vulnerable founder story connecting to mission
- Emotion: Passion, purpose, problem obsession
- CTA: "Join me for the launch. It means everything."
- Format: Text + founder photo
- Engagement Goal: Emotional connection, supporter mobilization

**Launch Day +1: Viral Moment**

**Post: "What We Learned in 24 Hours"**
- Hook: "Yesterday we launched [Product]. Here's what happened:"
- Content: Transparent metrics (signups, interesting patterns, surprises)
- Behind-scenes: "Serverscale issues at 2am, but team rallied"
- Learnings: "Top 3 things users are asking for"
- CTA: "We're listening. What do you want to see next?"
- Format: Text post with data
- Why it works: Transparency builds trust, building in public moment

**Launch Day +2: Comparison Content**

**Post: "[Product] vs [Alternative Approach]"**
- Hook: "Should you use [Product] or [existing solution]? Honest comparison:"
- Content: Fair, objective comparison chart
- When to use each: Specific scenarios
- Why it works: Addresses natural question, shows confidence
- CTA: "Questions about fit? DM me."
- Format: Comparison table carousel

**Launch Day +3: Founder Reflection**

**Post: "What I'd Tell Myself 6 Months Ago"**
- Hook: "Launching [Product] taught me [surprising lesson]."
- Content: Personal reflection on launch journey
- Vulnerability: What went wrong, what surprised founder
- Gratitude: Thank community, team, early customers
- CTA: "To everyone who believed in this: thank you."
- Format: Text + team photo
- Why it works: Humanizes brand, creates emotional connection

### Phase 3: Post-Launch Momentum (Weeks +1 to +3)

**Week +1: Customer Success Stories**

Focus: Shift from "we launched" to "customers are winning"

**Content Framework:**
- Day 1: "[Customer] implemented [Product] in 48 hours. Results: [metric]"
- Day 3: "Use case we didn't expect: How [Industry] is using [Product] for [surprising application]"
- Day 5: "We just hit [milestone: users, revenue, etc.]. Here's what we're learning:"

**Week +2: Product Education**

Focus: Address objections, dive into features, show advanced use cases

**Content Framework:**
- "Top 5 questions we're getting about [Product]‚Äîanswered"
- "You're probably not using [Feature] yet. Here's why you should:"
- "Advanced [Product] workflow that cuts [task] time by 80%"

**Week +3: Future Vision**

Focus: Show this is just the beginning, maintain momentum

**Content Framework:**
- "Based on your feedback, here's our Q2 roadmap:"
- "We're hiring [role]. Here's why this launch is just the start:"
- "What I learned launching [Product] that changes our next launch:"

### Engagement & Community Management Strategy

**Launch Week Comment Response Protocol:**

**First Hour of Each Post:**
- Respond to every comment within 15 minutes
- Ask follow-up questions to extend conversations
- Upvote insightful comments to surface them
- Tag relevant community members into discussions

**Comment Types & Responses:**

*Positive/Excited:*
"Love this, [Name]! Your [specific use case they mentioned] is exactly what we built for. DM me if you want early tips."

*Question/Confused:*
"Great question, [Name]. [Clear answer]. Does this help? Happy to elaborate on [specific aspect]."

*Skeptical/Critical:*
"Fair pushback, [Name]. Help me understand‚Äîare you seeing [their concern] with [current solution]? Here's how we address it: [explanation]"

*Competitor User:*
"We built [Product] for teams coming from [their tool]. Happy to show you a side-by-side. DM me?"

**Employee & Advocate Mobilization:**

Pre-launch brief to team/advisors/beta customers:
- "Here's our launch plan and how you can help"
- Provide shareable content snippets
- Request: Like, comment (substantive), and share key posts
- Timing: Share 2-4 hours after founder posts (not immediately‚Äîlooks coordinated)

**Real-Time Launch Dashboard:**

Track hourly on launch day, daily post-launch:

| Metric | Target | Actual | Status |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Waitlist/Signups | 1000 | - | - |
| LinkedIn Engagement | 5% avg | - | - |
| Profile Views | +500 | - | - |
| DM Conversations | 20 | - | - |
| Media Mentions | 3 | - | - |
| Partner Shares | 5 | - | - |

**Content Performance Tracking:**

| Post | Date | Type | Impressions | Engagement Rate | Clicks | Conversions |
|------|------|------|-------------|-----------------|--------|-------------|

**Identify Patterns:**
- Which posts drove most signups? (Optimize for that message/format)
- Which content got most shares? (Amplify similar content)
- Where did engagement drop? (Avoid those angles)
- What questions came up repeatedly? (Address in follow-up content)

**Week-Over-Week Review:**

- Pre-launch week: Did we hit waitlist targets? Build enough anticipation?
- Launch week: Did we hit conversion goals? What surprised us?
- Post-launch weeks: Is momentum sustaining or fading? What needs adjustment?

## Best Practices

- **Start Pre-Launch Campaign 4 Weeks Out**: Builds sufficient anticipation without fatigue
- **Progressive Revelation**: Don't show everything at once; tease, reveal, deepen across campaign
- **Founder Authenticity**: Personal stories outperform corporate announcements; be vulnerable and real
- **Social Proof Early and Often**: Beta tester quotes, case studies, and metrics build credibility
- **Engagement is Two-Way**: First hour of launch post is critical‚Äîbe present and responsive
- **Balance Hype and Substance**: Create excitement but deliver real value and honesty
- **Repurpose Across Channels**: LinkedIn content feeds email, blog, ads, and sales collateral
- **Plan for Pivots**: Market feedback may require messaging adjustments mid-campaign
- **Celebrate Team and Community**: Acknowledge supporters; creates loyalty and advocacy
- **Launch is Chapter 1, Not the Whole Book**: Sustain momentum post-launch; avoid the crash

## Common Pitfalls

- **Announcement Only, No Build-Up**: Posting launch day only; misses opportunity to build anticipation
- **Too Much Too Soon**: Revealing entire product week one; nothing left to tease
- **No Clear CTA**: Every post should have specific action; "check it out" is too vague
- **Ignoring Comments**: Posting without engaging; algorithm penalizes and community feels ignored
- **No Social Proof**: All claims, no validation; include testimonials and case studies
- **Founder MIA**: Team posts but founder silent; founder's voice is most authentic
- **One-Size-Fits-All Messaging**: Not segmenting for different stakeholders (customers vs. investors vs. press)
- **Post-Launch Silence**: Big launch then nothing; momentum dies immediately
- **No Measurement**: Can't tell what worked; repeat mistakes in next launch
- **Overpromising**: Hype that product can't deliver on; damages long-term trust
