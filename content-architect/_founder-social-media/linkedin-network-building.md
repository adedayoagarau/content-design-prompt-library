### Network Composition Strategy (Who to Connect With)

**The Network Architecture Framework:**

Effective networks aren't randomâ€”they're architected.

**Tier 1: Core Network (20% of connections, 80% of value)**

**Who:**
- Decision-makers in your ICP (can buy/partner/invest)

- Key influencers in your domain (amplification, credibility by association)
- Strategic advisors and mentors (guidance, introductions)
- Top talent prospects (future hires)

**Connection Criteria:**
- Direct relevance to **{{network_objective}}**
- Ability to provide or receive high value
- Active on LinkedIn (engagement potential)
- Shared context (industry, stage, geography, interests)

**Target Percentage**: 20% of total network

**Example for B2B SaaS Founder:**
- VPs of Sales at $10-100M companies (ICP)
- Fellow B2B SaaS founders Series A-B (peers)
- Sales/GTM influencers with 10K+ followers (amplification)
- SaaS investors and advisors (fundraising)
- Senior AEs and sales leaders (talent)

**Tier 2: Extended Network (50% of connections, 15% of value)**

**Who:**
- Relevant professionals one degree removed from ICP
- Industry practitioners (not decision-makers but connected to them)
- Vendors and service providers in your ecosystem
- Alumni, former colleagues, existing relationships
- Adjacent industries with crossover potential

**Value:**
- Amplification (they share/engage with your content)
- Connectors (can introduce to Tier 1)
- Market intelligence (trends, competitor insights)
- Potential future transitions into Tier 1

**Target Percentage**: 50% of total network

**Tier 3: Broad Network (30% of connections, 5% of value)**

**Who:**
- General professional connections
- Event attendees, conference contacts
- People who requested connection (not spam)
- Students, early-career professionals
- Passive connections from shared groups

**Value:**
- Weak ties for serendipity (unexpected opportunities)
- Diverse perspectives outside echo chamber
- Future potential (today's intern, tomorrow's VP)
- Baseline engagement for algorithm

**Target Percentage**: 30% of total network

**Strategic Implication:**

Track your network composition quarterly:
- Am I over-indexed on Tier 3? (Lots of connections, low business value)
- Am I neglecting Tier 1? (Missing target accounts)
- Is my network composition aligned to my business stage and goals?

### Strategic Connection Outreach System

**Finding Target Connections:**

**Method 1: LinkedIn Search (Most Targeted)**

Search operators:
```
"VP of Sales" OR "Head of Sales"
Industry: Software
Company size: 51-200
Location: United States
2nd degree (so you have mutual connection)
```

**Pro**: Precise targeting
**Con**: Time-intensive
**Best for**: Tier 1 strategic accounts

**Method 2: Competitor/Peer Follower Harvesting**

- Find influencer or competitor in your space
- View their followers (if public)
- Connect with relevant profiles

**Pro**: Pre-qualified audience (interested in your topic)
**Con**: Large accounts may have private follower lists
**Best for**: Expanding relevant Tier 2 network

**Method 3: Event-Based Networking**

- Conference attendee lists (if available)
- LinkedIn Live event participants
- Webinar registrants from your company
- Hashtag following (#SaaSGrowth participants)

**Pro**: Warm context ("saw you at X")
**Con**: Requires active event participation
**Best for**: Building genuine relationships with context

**Method 4: Mutual Connection Mining**

- Review your existing Tier 1 connections
- See their connections (2nd degree to you)
- Connect with relevant mutual connections

**Pro**: Social proof (mutual connection)
**Con**: Limited to your existing network's reach
**Best for**: Growing within specific industries/clusters

**Length**: 200 characters max (LinkedIn limit)
**Personalization**: Reference something specific about them
**Value**: Hint at relevance, don't pitch immediately

**No-Note Acceptable When:**
- 2nd degree connection (mutual connection implied context)
- They engaged with your content recently
- You're in same industry/clear relevance
- Mass connection after event you both attended

**Connection Request Volume:**

**Sustainable Pace**: 10-20 requests/week
- More than 20/week: Risks spam flags
- Less than 10/week: Slow growth

**Acceptance Rate Benchmarks:**
- Cold outreach with note: 20-40% acceptance
- No note: 10-20% acceptance
- After content engagement: 40-60% acceptance
- Post-event: 60-80% acceptance

**If acceptance rate <15%**: Your targeting is off or note is generic/spammy

### Post-Connection Relationship Nurturing

**The Biggest Mistake: Connect and Forget**

90% of LinkedIn users connect and never engage again.

**The Opportunity**: Systematic relationship development

**Relationship Development Ladder (Stranger â†’ Strategic Partner):**

**Stage 1: Stranger (Before connection)**
- Awareness: They see your content
- Action: Like/comment on their posts

**Stage 2: New Connection (Week 0)**
- Awareness: They accepted your request
- Action: Send thank you/context message (if appropriate)
- **Don't**: Immediately pitch your product/service

**Stage 3: Acquaintance (Weeks 1-4)**
- Awareness: They know who you are
- Action: Engage with their content (likes, thoughtful comments)
- Provide value: Share relevant article, make introduction, offer insight
- **Goal**: Stay on radar without being annoying

**Stage 4: Relationship (Months 2-3)**
- Awareness: They recognize your name, engage back
- Action: DM conversation, share content mutually, ask their opinion
- **Signal**: They like/comment on your posts, respond to DMs
- **Goal**: Reciprocal value exchange

**Stage 5: Strong Tie (Months 3-6+)**
- Awareness: They think of you for relevant opportunities
- Action: Regular communication, calls/meetings, collaborations
- **Signal**: They proactively reach out, make intros for you
- **Goal**: Mutual advocacy and business value

**Stage 6: Strategic Partner/Advocate (6+ months)**
- Awareness: Top-of-mind for relevant topics
- Action: Business transactions, ongoing partnership
- **Examples**: Customer, investor, advisor, co-marketing partner
- **Goal**: Measurable business outcomes

**Nurture Playbook by Tier:**

**Tier 1 (High-Priority) Nurture:**

- **Week 1**: Connection accepted â†’ Send context/thank you message
- **Week 2-4**: Like 2-3 of their posts, leave 1 thoughtful comment
- **Month 2**: Initiate DM with value-add (relevant article, intro, insight)
- **Month 3**: If engagement reciprocated, propose call or collaboration
- **Ongoing**: Touch point monthly minimum (comment, share, message)

**Tier 2 (Medium-Priority) Nurture:**

- **Week 1**: Connection accepted (no immediate message unless relevant)
- **Monthly**: Like their content, occasional thoughtful comment
- **Quarterly**: If relevant opportunity, reach out

**Tier 3 (Low-Priority) Nurture:**

- **Passive**: They see your content in feed
- **Reactive**: If they engage with you, engage back
- **No active nurturing** unless they move to Tier 2

**Time Allocation:**

If spending 45 min/day on LinkedIn:
- 15 min: Post and respond to own content
- 20 min: Tier 1 nurture (10-20 accounts, deep engagement)
- 10 min: Tier 2 nurture (broader feed engagement)

### Network Hygiene & Protection

**Who NOT to Connect With:**

âŒ **Obvious Spam:**
- Crypto/forex/MLM profiles
- "Life coach" with 50K followers but 10 engagement
- Profiles with no photo, sketchy bio
- Mass connection requesters

âŒ **Competitor Intel Fishing:**
- Competitors' sales reps clearly looking to poach
- Market research accounts mining information
- Recruiters for competitor companies (unless you want them to poach your team)

âŒ **Brand Risk:**
- Controversial figures that could damage association
- Direct competitors (if you share proprietary insights publicly)
- Known bad actors in your industry

**Connection Acceptance Criteria:**

âœ… **Accept When:**
- Relevant to your Tier 1/2/3 profiles
- Real person with legitimate profile
- Mutual connections or shared context
- They engaged with your content
- You attended same event

âš ï¸ **Evaluate:**
- No shared context but relevant industry
- Out of your geography but relevant role
- Very junior (could be future decision-maker)

**Managing Connection Requests:**

LinkedIn limits how many pending requests you can have (~100).

**Weekly Maintenance:**
- Review pending requests
- Accept aligned profiles
- Decline or ignore rest
- Withdraw old pending requests you sent

**Unfollowing vs. Disconnecting:**

**Unfollow** (Stay connected but hide from feed):
- Connections who post irrelevant content
- Over-posters clogging your feed
- Connections you want to keep but don't want to see

**Disconnect** (Rare):
- Spam accounts
- Someone who violated your trust
- Former employee/partner with bad relationship

**Privacy & Security:**

- **Profile Settings**: Decide who can see your connections, email, etc.
- **Activity Broadcasting**: Turn off if you don't want every profile visit/like broadcasted
- **Connection Visibility**: Consider making connections private (prevents harvesting)

## Best Practices

- **Quality Over Quantity**: 1,000 targeted connections > 10,000 random
- **Give Before You Get**: Provide value before asking for anything
- **Systematic Not Random**: Use tiers and tracking; don't let relationships decay
- **Personalize Outreach**: Reference something specific; generic requests get ignored
- **Patience Pays**: 3-6 months to convert cold connection to opportunity is normal
- **Track Relationships**: CRM/spreadsheet prevents forgetting promising connections
- **Regular Network Audits**: Quarterly review of composition and quality
- **Engage Don't Broadcast**: Commenting on others' content > posting only
- **Protect Your Brand**: Be selective about who you connect with; associations matter
- **Measure Business Outcomes**: Network size is vanity metric; opportunities/revenue is what matters

## Common Pitfalls

- **Connect and Pitch**: Immediate sales messages after connecting; burns relationships
- **Spray and Pray**: Sending 100 connection requests to anyone; low acceptance, low value
- **No Follow-Through**: Connecting but never engaging; wasted opportunities
